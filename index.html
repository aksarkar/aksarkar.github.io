Abhishek Sarkar

I am a Ph.D. student at Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Refer to my CV for more detailed information.

Research

My main research interests are the genotype-to-phenotype problem, epigenetics, regulatory genomics, systems genetics, and integrative analysis. I am particularly interested in developing novel algorithms, statistical methods, and software tools for dissecting the genetic basis of human disease.

Graduate

I currently work in the Computational Biology group with Manolis Kellis at the Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Lab.

My current work develops methods to query how genotype contributes to human disease. We integrate epigenetic information from the ENCODE and Roadmap Epigenomics consortia to identify cell-type–specific enhancers which show systematic bias in p-values for association with a variety of complex diseases. Moreover, these enrichments persist when considering thousands of independent loci.

Undergraduate

I worked in the UNC Computational Genetics Research Group with Wei Wang.

The broad focus of my work was on genetic recombination in model organism populations. I collaborated on the implementation of haplotype and ancestry inference tools which infer alleles from genotypes and inheritance of contiguous regions of the genome. In particular, I worked on a dynamic programming–based tool and an HMM-based tool for ancestry inference.
